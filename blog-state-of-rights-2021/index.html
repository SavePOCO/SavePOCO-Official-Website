The state of Right to Repair going into 2022...

Right to Repair has been a popular topic in the last quarter of 2021, and for good reason. With many advancements being made over the past few months in the tech space, with statements being issued by large companies in the tech industry, what is the state of our Right to Repair going into 2022?

Finally, some great news!

On the 17th of November, a large American tech brand issued a statement that brought the whole tech community joy. They announced that they will start embracing people's Right to Repair, starting with the last 2 generations of their smartphone line-up. These devices will allegedly be followed by the brand new line of laptops released.

Following this statement, this company will start providing parts, tools, and manuals to help consumers repair their own smartphones, and soon, the latest laptops. This is a great leap for us consumers, making it much easier to repair our devices. Along with this, customers will be issued an amount of credit for sending back the old, broken, or defective parts to them for recycling, which we think is an amazing idea.

The only gripe we have about this move by them is that parts, tools, and manuals have not been issued for older products with seemingly no plans to do so.

The European Union starts taking action!

Europe put new regulations regarding the Right to Repair into action in the first half of 2021. This included the long-lasting availability of spare parts for a specific set of home appliances. The list of appliances currently is short, however, it gives us hope for expansion in the future.

The appliances on this list are:

  • Washing Machines
  • Dishwashers
  • Refrigerators
  • Displays

    • According to these new regulations, all the appliances above must have spare parts readily available with repair documentation. However, consumers may not be able to have access to either of these, as it seems they are limited to professional third-party repair centres. Manufacturers must be able to supply these repair centres with spare parts within 15 business days. Alongside that, based on the type of product, manufacturers must support these appliances for as long as 7 to even 10 years. These rules have been applied to the countries in the European Union, along with the UK.

    The verdict.

    In conclusion, we can say that the situation regarding our Right to Repair has improved over the course of 2021, but there are still plenty of issues to be acknowledged. Let's hope for 2022 to be an even better year, and to bring our Rights to Repair farther than ever before!
